-1Anxiety (GAD)
"Anxiety" is a catch-all term for a number of different Anxiety Disorders.
It usually refers to Generalized Anxiety Disorder (GAD). 
GAD involves a persistent feeling of dread that interferes with daily life.
-2Symptoms
The National Institute of Mental Health lists the following symptoms of GAD:
+Feeling restless, wound-up, or on-edge
+Being easily fatigued
+Having difficulty concentrating
+Being irritable
+Having headaches, muscle aches, stomachaches, or unexplained pains
+Difficulty controlling feelings of worry
+Having sleep problems, such as difficulty falling or staying asleep
-2Causes
+Stressful and/or negative life experiences
+Family history of anxiety or other mental disorders
+Certain physical health conditions - thyroid problems, heart arrhythmia
+Caffeine or other substances and medications
-2Treatment
+Psychotherapy
++Cognitive Behavioral Therapy (CBT) focuses on how to think about stress and fear
++Acceptance and Commitment Therapy (ACT) focuses on mindfulness and goal-setting to reduce anxiety
+Medication - not a cure, but can help with symptoms
++Antidepressants can help calm your brain chemistry and return it to a less stressful equilibrium
++Anti-anxiety Medications can help reduce the symptoms of anxiety and panic attacks, or extreme fear and worry
-2Other Anxiety Disorders
GAD is not the only form of anxiety, here are some of the most common and their characteristics:
+Panic Disorder
++Frequent and sudden panic attacks - periods of intense fear, discomfort, or sense of losing control
+Social Anxiety Disorder
++Intense, persistent fear of being watched and judged by others
+Phobia-related disorders
++A phobia is an intense fear of certain objects or situations
